3 Reasons Why Every Mental Health Specialist Needs a Website

Last updated Sep. 4, 2020


The year 2020 has been a challenging one, to say the least. Particularly, in the UK. With Brexit and COVID-19 combining forces to ensure that this year is one that will be especially difficult to overcome for people suffering from mental illnesses.


I have no hard evidence here but it seems that cases would have only surged since the outbreak started. Additionally, cases that already were, would have likely been exacerbated further.


If there really has been a huge increase in mental illness cases recently. Then that can only mean, demand for mental health support is very high. Maybe higher now than for a long time.


However, a higher demand can only be met with a higher supply. Even if there are enough mental health specialists out there. A huge chunk of them still don't even have a website in 2020 (trust me, I know, I've looked around).


The first place people will look for help is on the internet. If you're not there, your potential client base doesn't know you exist. So, how can you help?


Whether you're a counsellor, psychiatrist, or hypnotherapist. Every mental health specialist should have a website in 2020.


1. Being There



Whether, you as a mental health specialist, consider your service as an independent business or not, does not entirely matter. The fact is, 81% of people research a product or service online before making a purchasing decision.


If you’re not online and your competitors are, can you really expect to win the client the majority of the time?


Unlikely.


By having a website, you’re not only ‘just there’, but you’re there every day, 7 days a week. Having a website is almost like having your own agent.


Except this robot agent doesn't eat, drink, or sleep.


Pretty handy right?


When you’re sleeping, your website is still there, warming up and generating leads. When you’re spending time with your family, your website will still be there. Even when you’re on a two week holiday in Spain, your website will still be working for you.


This point alone should be enough of a reason for you to get serious about having a website. But lets continue...


2. Ensure Trust and Professionalism



I hate to be the one to break it to you (no I don't), you need a website to be taken seriously by potential clients and rightfully so.


In fact, 56% of people stated that they don’t trust a business without a website.


Are you genuinely content with over half of the population not trusting your business/service?


I wouldn't be.


In the year 2020, an overwhelming majority of business is done on the internet. If you’re not on there by now, are you really taking your business/service seriously?


If the answer is no. Then, why should anybody else?


They shouldn’t.


Having a website, shows you care. It shows you care about your business/service enough to go out and make your service be known to the general public.


And if people notice that you care, then you become a lot easier to trust.


3. Save Time



Many small business owners (without a website) give off the impression that having a website consumes more time than not having one.


On the contrary. Having a website is a huuuge time saver.


It simply requires a time investment upfront.


An effective website allows you to have sections specifically designed for saving time. Such as, a testimonial and FAQ section.


As I alluded to earlier, merely having a website ensures more trust than not having a website. But having a testimonial section where former clients are telling the world how great you are?


That'll win you a serious amount of trust points with people on the fence about purchasing your service.


Ensuring trust with a testimonial actually saves you a lot of time because it allows you to avoid endless questions from people unsure about your service. Because former clients have already answered those questions for them.


An FAQ section would have a similar effect. Except that you, yourself, would be answering frequently asked questions. Clearing any room for doubt that’s clogging the way between your potential client's scepticism and he/she purchasing your service.


A blog on your website is also a good way to answer questions and clarify any misconceptions about your service.


Which is what I'm doing right now :)
